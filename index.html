Why I'm in the LaunchCode 101 Program

I'm self-taught in many areas, and lack formal credentials or recent work experience that would help "legitimize" some of my skills. LaunchCode is providing a valuable service by helping match companies and job candidates.

So why start with LC 101, rather than immediately apply for an apprenticeship?

Big Hat . . . No Project

I've done programming 'in the small' for years, but have spent less than 15% of my working life as a full-time developer — and those experiences were spread over several years. The "bigger" stuff I've done has been proprietary, so can't be shared as evidence of job-readiness.

I need to build a reasonable project, and open-source it as evidence of my abilities. I thought it would be good to complete LC 101 for its range of technologies and collaborative opportunities. I had started working on CS50x, but really wanted to participate in an in-person course.
